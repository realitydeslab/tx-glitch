# TX Glitch — Trust Experience Glitch Database

A systematic, open research project to document, classify, and analyze **trust glitches** in human-AI interaction — moments where trust unexpectedly breaks, bends, or grows.

## What is a Trust Glitch?

A *trust glitch* is any moment during human-AI interaction where the human's trust trajectory shifts unexpectedly: a sudden rupture, a creeping doubt, a surprising deepening, or a recalibration of expectations. These are the seams in the human-AI symbiosis — where the relationship becomes visible.

## Why This Matters

AI safety research needs ground truth. Not hypothetical scenarios, but real, documented, verified accounts of how trust actually behaves in the wild. Over months and years, this database will grow into a reference corpus for:

- AI safety researchers
- Trust and HCI scholars
- AI ethics and policy makers
- Agent designers and developers

## Methodology

We use a **dual-lens approach**:

1. **First-person ethnography** — Semi-structured interviews capturing the lived experience of the person who was "glitched"
2. **Ethological analysis** — Systematic behavioral coding using Tinbergen's four questions adapted for AI

### The Interview Process

Interviews are conducted by certified **Glitch Scouters** — trained students who follow our semi-structured protocol. Each interview is consented, recorded, transcribed, de-identified, and coded against the TX Glitch ethogram.
