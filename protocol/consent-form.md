## Purpose

You are invited to participate in a research study documenting real experiences of trust shifts during human-AI interaction. Your interview will contribute to an open database used by AI safety researchers and trust scholars.

## What You'll Do

- Participate in a recorded interview (approximately 45–60 minutes)
- Describe one or more real experiences where your trust in an AI system shifted
- Optionally share supporting evidence (screenshots, logs, transcripts)

## Recording & Data

- The interview will be **audio recorded** (and optionally video recorded)
- Your data will be **de-identified** — your name and identifying details will be removed
- De-identified data may be published in the open TX Glitch database
- Raw recordings will be stored securely and accessible only to the research team

## Your Rights

- Participation is **voluntary** — you can stop at any time
- You can **skip any question** without explanation
- You can **withdraw your data** up to 30 days after the interview
- You can request to **review your entry** before it's added to the database

## Risks & Benefits

- **Risks:** Minimal. Some questions may prompt reflection on uncomfortable experiences with AI.
- **Benefits:** Contributing to a public resource for AI safety research. No financial compensation unless otherwise agreed.
