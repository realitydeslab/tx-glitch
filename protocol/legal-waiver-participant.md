## 1. Purpose of This Study

Trust.Fail collects real accounts of trust shifts ("glitches") in human-AI interaction through semi-structured interviews. Your interview will contribute to an open research database used by AI safety researchers, academic institutions, and industry.

## 2. What You Are Agreeing To

By signing this waiver, you consent to:

### 2.1 The Interview
- Participating in a semi-structured interview (approximately 30–60 minutes)
- Being interviewed by either a certified Glitch Scouter (human) or an AI interviewer agent
- The interview being conducted via text chat, voice call, or in person

### 2.2 Recording
- The interview will be **recorded** (audio, video, and/or text log depending on medium)
- The recording exists **solely as a verifiable record** of what was said
- The recording will **NOT** be published, shared, or used for direct analysis
- The recording will be stored securely and accessible only to the research team
- Recordings will be retained for a period of **3 years** after the interview, then destroyed unless you consent to extended retention

### 2.3 The Ethnographic Card
- A structured summary ("ethnographic card") will be generated from your interview
- This card will be **de-identified** — your name, employer, and identifying details will be removed or anonymized
- You will have the opportunity to **review the card** before it is published
- Once confirmed, the ethnographic card will be published in the open Trust.Fail database

### 2.4 Publication & Open Data
- Your de-identified ethnographic card will be published under a **Creative Commons Attribution 4.0 (CC BY 4.0)** license
- This means anyone — researchers, companies, journalists, the public — may read, cite, and build upon your data
- Your card will be assigned a permanent identifier and may be cited in academic publications
- You understand that once published, de-identified data **cannot be fully retracted** from copies others may have made

## 3. Data Privacy

### 3.1 What Data We Collect
- **Interview content:** Your spoken or written responses during the interview
- **Recording:** Audio, video, or text log of the interview session
- **Metadata:** Interview date, duration, medium, interviewer/Scouter ID
- **Contact information** (optional): Email or other contact for follow-up, stored separately from interview data
- **Consent records:** This signed waiver

### 3.2 What Data We Publish
- **Only the de-identified ethnographic card.** This contains:
  - The AI system involved (name, type, platform)
  - The glitch event (setting, behavior, expectation, deviation)
  - Your emotional response and trust trajectory (anonymized)
  - Ethological analysis
  - Your own name for the glitch type
  - Notable quotes (de-identified)
  - Interviewer observations (de-identified)

### 3.3 What Data We Do NOT Publish
- Your name, employer, or any identifying information
- The raw recording
- Your contact information
- Any content you request to be excluded during the review phase

### 3.4 Data Storage & Security
- Raw recordings are stored on encrypted, access-controlled storage
- Only the Principal Investigator and authorized research team members can access raw recordings
- De-identified ethnographic cards are stored in a public GitHub repository
- Contact information is stored separately from interview data, in an encrypted database
- All data handling complies with **UK GDPR** and the **UK Data Protection Act 2018**

### 3.5 Third-Party AI Processing
- If your interview is conducted by an AI agent, your conversation will be processed by a third-party AI service (e.g., OpenAI, Anthropic) to facilitate the interview and generate the ethnographic card
- These services process data according to their respective privacy policies and data processing agreements
- We use API configurations that **do not** allow your data to be used for model training
- If voice is used, audio is processed by a speech-to-text service; audio is not retained by the service after transcription

### 3.6 Data Retention
| Data Type | Retention Period | Location |
|-----------|-----------------|----------|
| Raw recording | 3 years, then destroyed | Encrypted storage |
| Contact information | Until study completion or withdrawal | Encrypted database |
| De-identified ethnographic card | Indefinite (open data) | Public GitHub repo |
| Consent records | 5 years after study completion | Encrypted storage |
| AI interview transcripts | Until card is confirmed, then deleted | Encrypted storage |

### 3.7 International Data Transfers
- The Trust.Fail database is hosted on GitHub (servers in the US)
- If you are in the UK/EU, your de-identified data will be transferred to the US under Standard Contractual Clauses
- Raw recordings and identifying data remain on UK/EU-based encrypted storage

## 4. Your Rights

Under UK GDPR, you have the right to:

- **Access:** Request a copy of all data we hold about you
- **Rectification:** Correct any inaccuracies in your data
- **Erasure:** Request deletion of your data (subject to limitations below)
- **Withdrawal:** Withdraw your consent at any time without giving a reason
- **Restriction:** Request that we stop processing your data
- **Portability:** Receive your data in a machine-readable format
- **Objection:** Object to processing of your data
- **Complaint:** Lodge a complaint with the UK Information Commissioner's Office (ICO)

### 4.1 Withdrawal
- You may withdraw from the study **at any time** by contacting the research team
- If you withdraw **before** your ethnographic card is published: all your data (recording, transcript, card) will be deleted
- If you withdraw **after** publication: we will remove the card from the live database. However, we cannot guarantee removal from copies others may have already made (the database is open and licensed under CC BY 4.0)
- Withdrawal deadline for guaranteed full deletion: **30 days after the interview**

### 4.2 Review Before Publication
- You will be sent your ethnographic card for review before publication
- You may request changes, redactions, or additional de-identification
- You may reject the card entirely — in which case it will not be published
- If you do not respond within **14 days** of receiving the card, we will follow up once; if no response after **30 days total**, the card will be published as-is

## 5. Risks

### 5.1 Minimal Risks
- Some questions may prompt reflection on uncomfortable or frustrating experiences with AI
- You are free to skip any question or stop the interview at any time

### 5.2 Re-identification Risk
- Although we de-identify all data, there is a small risk that a particularly unique or detailed glitch event could be re-identified by someone with knowledge of the situation
- We mitigate this by: removing names, employers, and specific identifiers; generalizing details where possible; allowing you to review and request further anonymization

### 5.3 Third-Party AI Risk
- If using the AI interviewer, your conversation passes through third-party AI infrastructure
- We mitigate this by using enterprise/API configurations that do not train on your data

## 6. Benefits

- Contributing to a public research resource for AI safety
- Helping researchers and designers understand how trust works (and fails) in human-AI interaction
- No financial compensation unless otherwise agreed in a separate arrangement
