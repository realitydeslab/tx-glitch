## 1. Your Role

As a certified Glitch Scouter, you are an authorized interviewer and data handler for the Trust.Fail project. You will:

- Conduct semi-structured interviews with participants
- Handle sensitive personal data (recordings, transcripts, contact information)
- Generate, review, and confirm ethnographic cards
- Upload verified data to the Trust.Fail database

This agreement defines your responsibilities, obligations, and liabilities.

## 2. What You Are Agreeing To

### 2.1 Training & Certification
- You have completed the Trust.Fail Scouter training programme
- You understand and can follow the semi-structured interview protocol
- You can code interviews against the ethogram coding scheme
- You understand the ethical principles of qualitative research with human participants

### 2.2 Data Handling Obligations
You agree to:

- **Follow the protocol** — conduct all interviews according to the Trust.Fail semi-structured interview protocol
- **Obtain consent** — ensure every participant has signed the Participant Legal Waiver before the interview begins
- **De-identify rigorously** — remove all identifying information (names, employers, locations, unique identifiers) from ethnographic cards before submission
- **Secure raw data** — store recordings and transcripts on encrypted devices; never on unencrypted personal devices, cloud drives, or shared computers
- **Never share raw data** — recordings, transcripts, and contact information must never be shared with anyone outside the authorized research team
- **Delete promptly** — delete local copies of recordings and transcripts within 7 days of the ethnographic card being confirmed and uploaded
- **Report breaches** — immediately report any data breach, loss, or unauthorized access to the Principal Investigator

### 2.3 Ethical Obligations
You agree to:

- **Treat participants with respect** — create a safe, non-judgmental interview space
- **Never lead or coerce** — do not suggest answers, pressure participants, or steer narratives
- **Respect withdrawal** — if a participant wants to stop, stop immediately. No questions, no pressure
- **Maintain confidentiality** — never discuss participant identities or raw interview content outside the research team
- **Flag concerns** — if a participant discloses distress, harm, or illegal activity, follow the escalation protocol (Section 4)
- **Declare conflicts** — disclose any personal or professional relationship with a participant before conducting an interview
- **No fabrication** — never fabricate, embellish, or alter interview data. The ethnographic card must faithfully represent what the participant said

### 2.4 Intellectual Property
- Ethnographic cards you produce are contributed to the Trust.Fail project under CC BY 4.0
- You will be credited as interviewer (by Scouter ID) in the card metadata
- You may cite and reference Trust.Fail data in your own academic work with proper attribution
- You may not independently publish raw interview data or recordings

### 2.5 Certification Maintenance
- You must conduct at least 2 interviews per month to maintain active status
- You must participate in monthly calibration sessions
- Your inter-rater reliability must remain at κ ≥ 0.7
- Certification may be suspended or revoked for protocol violations, data breaches, ethical misconduct, or sustained inactivity

## 3. Data Protection Responsibilities

### 3.1 You Are a Data Processor
Under UK GDPR, you act as a **data processor** on behalf of the Trust.Fail project (the data controller). This means:

- You may only process participant data for the purposes of this study
- You must follow the data handling instructions in this agreement
- You must not use participant data for any other purpose
- You must assist the research team in responding to data subject access requests

### 3.2 Technical Security Requirements
| Requirement | Minimum Standard |
|-------------|-----------------|
| Device encryption | Full-disk encryption enabled |
| Password protection | Strong password or biometric on all devices storing data |
| Recording storage | Encrypted folder/vault on local device |
| Transfer method | Encrypted upload to project storage (not email, not WhatsApp) |
| Cloud storage | Do NOT store raw data on personal cloud (Google Drive, iCloud, Dropbox) |
| Deletion | Secure deletion (not just moving to trash) within 7 days of card confirmation |

### 3.3 What You Must Never Do
- ❌ Store raw recordings on unencrypted devices
- ❌ Send recordings or transcripts via email, messaging apps, or social media
- ❌ Share participant identities with anyone outside the research team
- ❌ Access raw data after your certification is revoked or expired
- ❌ Use participant data for personal research without explicit written permission
- ❌ Discuss identifiable participant details in any public forum (including academic conferences, unless fully de-identified)

## 4. Escalation Protocol

### 4.1 Participant Distress
If a participant becomes visibly distressed during an interview:
1. Pause the interview
2. Ask if they want to continue, take a break, or stop
3. Remind them they can skip any question or withdraw entirely
4. If they wish to stop, end the interview with compassion
5. Log the incident in your interviewer notes
6. If you believe the participant may be at risk of harm, contact the PI immediately

### 4.2 Disclosure of Harm
If a participant discloses that they or someone else has been harmed (physically, emotionally, financially) as a result of AI interaction:
1. Listen without judgment
2. Do not promise confidentiality for disclosures involving risk of harm to self or others
3. Contact the PI within 24 hours
4. The PI will determine whether external reporting is required (e.g., to safeguarding authorities)

### 4.3 Disclosure of Illegal Activity
If a participant describes using AI for illegal purposes:
1. Do not encourage or probe further into the illegal activity
2. Redirect to the trust experience itself
3. Contact the PI within 24 hours
4. The PI will seek legal advice on reporting obligations

### 4.4 Data Breach
If you suspect or discover a data breach (lost device, unauthorized access, accidental sharing):
1. **Immediately** notify the PI (within 24 hours maximum)
2. Document what happened, what data was affected, and what actions you've taken
3. The PI will assess whether notification to the ICO is required (UK GDPR mandates notification within 72 hours for qualifying breaches)
