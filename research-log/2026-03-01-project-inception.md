# 2026-03-01 — Project Inception

## Core Idea

Build a systematic database of **Trust Experience Glitches** — moments where trust between humans and AI agents shifts unexpectedly. This will serve as ground truth for AI safety researchers, trust scholars, and agent designers.

## Key Design Decisions

### Why not self-reporting?
Self-reporting is prone to its own "glitches" — people fabricate, embellish, or misremember. The interview method provides:
- Verification through trained interviewer probing
- Recorded evidence (audio/video)
- Contractual commitment to honesty
- Second-person observational layer

### Dual analytical lens
1. **First-person ethnography** — lived experience, thick description, phenomenology
2. **Ethological analysis** — Tinbergen's four questions adapted for AI behavior:
   - **Mechanism:** What architecture/training caused the behavior?
   - **Development:** How did the behavior emerge over the interaction history?
   - **Function:** What purpose does the behavior serve (if any)?
   - **Phylogeny:** What design lineage does the system come from?

### Glitch Scouters
Certified student interviewers. The certification process itself builds trust and ensures data quality. Scouters can:
- Conduct interviews following the semi-structured protocol
- Code and upload interview data
- Serve as the human quality layer between raw experience and database entry

## Analogies

- **NASA ASRS** — Aviation Safety Reporting System. Voluntary, confidential, no-blame incident reporting that transformed aviation safety. TX Glitch is the ASRS for human-AI trust.
- **Ethograms in animal behavior** — Standardized behavioral catalogs. We're building the ethogram for trust behavior in human-AI interaction.

## Open Questions

- What's the right taxonomy of glitch types? Let it emerge from data (grounded theory) or seed with categories?
- How to handle culturally specific trust norms?
- Privacy: some glitch events may involve sensitive AI outputs
- Scale: how many interviews before the database is useful? (Probably 50+ for initial patterns)
- Website design: how to balance open submission with quality control?
