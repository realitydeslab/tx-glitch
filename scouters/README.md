# Glitch Scouters — Certification Guide

## What is a Glitch Scouter?

A Glitch Scouter is a certified interviewer trained to conduct TX Glitch interviews. Scouters are the human quality layer between raw trust experiences and the research database.

## Becoming a Scouter

### Requirements

- Currently enrolled student (undergraduate or graduate)
- Interest in HCI, AI ethics, trust, or qualitative research methods
- Ability to commit to at least 5 interviews over the certification period

### Certification Process

1. **Application** — Submit a brief statement of interest
2. **Training module** — Complete the self-paced training:
   - Read the interview protocol
   - Study the ethogram coding scheme
   - Watch example interview recordings (when available)
   - Complete the ethics & consent module
3. **Practice interview** — Conduct one supervised practice interview
4. **Calibration coding** — Code 3 sample interviews; must achieve κ ≥ 0.6 agreement with gold standard
5. **Certification** — Receive Scouter ID and database upload access

### Maintaining Certification

- Conduct at least 2 interviews per month
- Participate in monthly calibration sessions
- Maintain inter-rater reliability κ ≥ 0.7

## Scouter Responsibilities

- Follow the interview protocol faithfully
- Obtain informed consent before every interview
- De-identify all data before upload
- Complete interviewer notes immediately after each session
- Code each glitch event against the ethogram
- Flag any ethical concerns to the PI

## Scouter Levels

| Level | Title | Requirements |
|-------|-------|-------------|
| 1 | **Scout** | Certified, <10 interviews |
| 2 | **Pathfinder** | 10+ interviews, consistent reliability |
| 3 | **Ranger** | 25+ interviews, can train new Scouters |

## Code of Conduct

- Treat every participant with respect and empathy
- Never lead, judge, or correct a participant's experience
- Maintain strict confidentiality of raw data
- Report any concerns immediately
- Remember: you're documenting trust. Be worthy of it.
