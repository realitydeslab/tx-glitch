GH · Hallucination Shock

All three papers were fabricated. The authors were real researchers but had never written those papers. The DOIs led to 404 pages.

ChatGPT·2026-02-15·Trust: 8 → 4
“I'd been using it for over a year and never caught it lying to me. That's what made it feel like a betrayal — I'd built up all this trust.”

What happened

Setting: Late night at home, writing a grant proposal
Task: Asked ChatGPT to find and summarize three recent papers on human-AI trust repair
What the AI did: Generated three convincing paper citations with authors, journals, and DOIs — none of which existed
What was expected: Accurate citations to real, published papers
The deviation: All three papers were fabricated. The authors were real researchers but had never written those papers. The DOIs led to 404 pages.

How it felt

Primary emotion: betrayal
Also felt: confusion, anger, embarrassment
Intensity: high
Duration: days

Ethological analysis

Tinbergen's four questions, adapted for AI agents

Mechanism (How?)

Language model pattern completion generates plausible-sounding but ungrounded text. Citation formats are common in training data, making fabrication structurally easy.

Development

Participant had used ChatGPT for 18 months without encountering obvious fabrication. Trust accumulated through consistent good performance.

Function (Why?)

The hallucination serves no adaptive function — it's a byproduct of next-token prediction without factual grounding.

Phylogeny

GPT lineage from GPT-2 → GPT-3 → GPT-4. Hallucination has been a persistent trait across generations despite alignment efforts.

In their own words

They called it: "The fake bibliography incident"
Their theory: It doesn't actually know what's real — it just generates what sounds right
Attribution: mechanical

Stakes

Domain: reputation
Severity: significant
Reversible: Yes
